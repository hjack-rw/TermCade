# Modern Era Villains

A side is not just one person. When the Xiaolin raised its own – the elemental Dragons – the Heylin
raised Chase Young, who was given enough power to stand where Dashi once stood.

What neither side can do any more is put all of itself into one pair of hands. That height is in the
Wu now, and it stays there as long as they are out of reach.

---

> **Chase Young**

He was Dashi's pupil once, alongside his companion and friend Monk Guan, and he was destined for
greatness – perhaps to surpass even Dashi himself. What undid him was the fear of losing his
Master's favour to Guan. He went to a Heylin demon, Hannibal Roy Bean, for help turning himself evil, 
and then betrayed Bean, locking him in the spiritual Yin-Yang World. If he ever gets out, Hannibal *will definitely be angry*.

The turning gave Chase powers to rival his former Master's. He turns the foes he beats into predatory cat creatures
that serve him, and he can take the shape of a monstrous beast himself. What he *wants now is unclear*, however.
Perhaps a pupil of his own – he certainly has an eye out for Omi.

He despises the use of Shen Gong Wu, maybe because they remind him of Dashi.
Often he enters a Showdown for nothing else but the lust of the fight.

---

> **Wuya**

Released by the self-proclaimed young genius Jack Spicer, what came out of the box was a ghost of her former
self. Wuya's body was gone. What was left was a voice and an appetite.

Because the Wu were made from her own essence, she could feel them **wake**. She felt the need to
consume them too – to take the power that had been hers to begin with back into herself.

---

> **Chase Young's terms**

After a fight with Spicer, Wuya left him and struck a deal with Chase. He gave her old body back with the help of
the Monarch Wings, and she became **solid** again. But Chase did not trust her. He didn't help her gain all her former powers back – those are still in the Wu, still scattered, still anybody's to take.

With a body she could at least cast spells again. Something cruder: green plasma thrown like fire, and empty
husk golems with nothing behind their eyes.

But Chase forbids it. He calls it cheating, and finds it beneath **his** own practice of the Heylin.
So she walks again, *weaker than she has ever been*, and she does what he tells her.
