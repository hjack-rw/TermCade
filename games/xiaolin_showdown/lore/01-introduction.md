# Introduction

Grand Master Dashi was a skilled martial artist and a brilliant craftsman, chosen as the avatar of the
Xiaolin in this world. For as long as he held that place, he protected the lands from the Heylin Witch
Wuya.

She was not one witch among many. Her power had been handed to her, and she meant to conquer the
world with it. She embodied the worst of the Heylin as Dashi embodied the best of the Xiaolin – the
two highest things either side had ever made, locked in a battle neither of them could finish. Dashi
could not beat her, not as they stood. His power had been earned through training, and earning is
slower.

So he took hers away.

He came up with a since forgotten technique to **strip someone of their supernatural power**, and
used it on her. It did not destroy her. It made her **weaker**, and weak was enough – he locked
her in a puzzle box, and the world had fifteen hundred quiet years.

The powers he took had to go somewhere, and what he had to put them in were his own crafted toys.
Those are the things that would later be known as **Shen Gong Wu**.

Taking her power out of the world left it unbalanced: leave one avatar standing and the scale tips 
the other way, and that is not a victory, it is the same catastrophe wearing the winner's face. 
Both Masters had to go out of the world at the same time, so that neither side could reach that high again. 
Therefore he stripped his own powers too. 

---

> **The Shen Gong Wu**

They do not break easily, and power cannot simply leave the world – so the Wu had to be put beyond
easy reach instead. They were scattered across it, and nobody was better suited to the job than
Dashi's sidekick and trusted companion dragon, Dojo Kanojo Cho.

Nobody ever named them; nobody got the chance. So a Wu is named by whoever rediscovers it, and that is
mostly Dojo, remembering where he hid them as they wake and go active again after their long slumber.
The names stuck by repetition rather than by decree, and many Wu answer to more than one of them.

Some in the pile have never been named at all, still waiting their turn.

A Wu answers to anyone who picks it up. Some of them are made from Heylin power and some from Xiaolin,
and neither cares whose hand it lands in. Dashi is said to have wanted it that way – *a power that
only served one side would not have balanced anything*.

---

> **Consequences**

A power trapped in an object reshapes it. Most of the time that is a small thing –
a detail, a quirk, an edge nobody asked for. Sometimes it is the whole of the Wu: the Sapphire Dragon
is one enormous side effect, and what was poured into it was Wuya's own malice.

Not every consequence was foreseen. Bring enough Wu together, combine them with the core – the Heart
of Jong – and a construct comes forward, bound to whoever assembled him. He has almost a mind of his
own, and few can truly control him. The Xiaolin Monks fear his name: Mala Mala Jong.
