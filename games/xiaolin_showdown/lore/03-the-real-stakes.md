# Real Stakes

Every Wu that surfaces goes one of two ways.

**You secure it.** Into the vault, out of reach, out of play – where no hand that wants the world can
close on it. That is the whole mission of the Xiaolin warriors, and it is not glamorous work. You are not
collecting. You are *removing*.

**They take it.** The Heylin do not secure anything. They gather, because every Wu they hold is one
more piece of the power to rule the world, and they have never wanted it for any reason more
sophisticated than that.

---

> **What is at stake**

If they gather enough, they will be too strong to stop. And what follows is **a thousand years of
darkness**, which is a long enough time that nobody who could have stopped it will be alive to be sorry.

That is what the race is for. Not a trophy shelf. **A world that still has a morning in it.**

---

> **Why is it not a hoard**

A Wu you are carrying is a Wu you can lose – staked, stolen, torn out of your hands in a Showdown you
misjudged. It is only safe once it is **vaulted**, and vaulting it means giving up its power. 
You cannot wield a Wu and secure it at the same time. You choose, and you choose one at a time.

Gather enough and the balance holds.

---

> **What you set aside**

One hand can only hold so much. The rest you need to stash – buried, hidden, pressed into the keeping of someone
you hope you can trust.

This is **not** securing it. It is putting a Wu somewhere out of your hands and out of your reach, 
on the promise that you will come back for it when you have room. You can go back for it sure. It
costs you the same time as anything else does, and the hunt does not wait.

But if the hunt ends while it is still out there, it does not count. The race is over, and what you carried
home is all you have secured.
