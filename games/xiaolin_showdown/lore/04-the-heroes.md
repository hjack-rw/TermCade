# Xiaolin Dragons

Four are chosen to train under Master Monks in the Temple. Water, fire, wind, earth – one for each element. 
It's not a costume. It is what they *are*.

But a Dragon is not born in command of their element. A Xiaolin monk needs to study to reach their full potential.

---

> **The Elements**

Five elements, and they do not sit in a circle politely.

**Water** puts out **fire**, and **fire** evaporates **water**. **Wind** scours **earth**,
and **earth** blocks **wind**. Each of those four has something it beats and something that beats it, 
and a Wu carries the temperament of whatever it was forged from.

**Metal** answers to nothing and nothing answers to it. On any ground that has a temper of its own, 
metal is the wrong tool – it does not agree with the place it is standing in, and the place does not
help it in any way, shape or form. Neutral ground is the only place it belongs. 
That is the bargain: a common Wu is reliable everywhere and *favoured* almost nowhere.

---

> **The Wudai Amulets**

When that study is done, the amulet is what opens the door to true greatness. It is an Elemental Shen
Gong Wu, part of the Wudai Warriors outfit, and it is what gives a Dragon their power: it lets them
command the element they embody.

So it is not a Wu in the classic way the others are Wu. It cannot be staked, because it was never a prize; 
you would be wagering your own element.

It cannot be secured either. There is no vaulting it. Where you go, it goes.
